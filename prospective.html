For Prospective Graduate Students

Am I taking students?

Thanks for your interest! I'll be recruiting a small number of graduate students this admissions cycle, to start working together in Fall 2023. I'm particularly interested in working on language grounding, interactive systems, AI-assisted programming, and pragmatics.

How to apply?

The best way to work with me (and many great folks) is to apply to LTI's PhD and/or MLT programs. The application deadline for this year is December 12th, 2022 3pm EST. There's more information here.

Should you email me?

I'd love to be able to reply to every email I get about admissions, but I just don't have the capacity, unfortunately.

It's not necessary to email me -- I plan to look at all applications to LTI's PhD and MLT programs that mention my name or my research interests in the statement of purpose. (At LTI, faculty read applications, but we don't unilaterally admit students, so you should definitely give a complete and specific description of your interests in your SoP.) That being said, LTI gets a lot of applications, and if you're worried your application might get overlooked it's totally fine to also email me to tell me that you've applied and are interested in working together, so long as we have common research interests and you describe the overlap in our interests in your email. (I can't speak for other faculty, but I'm personally fine with getting these types of tailored emails.) Unfortunately, I can't generally respond even to these tailored emails about admissions (I just get too many emails), sorry! But I do really appreciate your interest and time applying.

Yonatan Bisk has a page with more context about the application process.
